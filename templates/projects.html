3. Classification of Independent Medical Examination Reports

This project aims to use de-sensitized medical documents to develop text mining-based classification models. At the request of an orthopedic expert, our goal is to categorize approximately 5000 independent medical examiner reports into one of three categories based on the status of the patient’s injury: permanent, not permanent, or unsure. The task involves developing an automated system to process the physician’s free-text notes to infer their analysis of the patient’s condition.

Students
  • Cole Pearson
Mentor
  • Dr. Naeem Seliya

4. Named Entity Recognition in Unstructured Medical Text Documents

Independent medical examiner reports contain personally identifiable information that needs to be removed or randomly encoded before further research work can be conducted. The goal of this research is to perform named entity recognition, a type of natural language processing, to identify and subsequently remove/encode PII information from IME reports prepared by the physician. We apply the NER toolkits of OpenNLP and spaCy, two freely available NLP platforms.

Students
  • Cole Pearson
Mentor
  • Dr. Naeem Seliya

5. User Authentication Schemes Using Machine Learning Methods – A Review

Physical layer authentication has emerged as an effective continuous authentication method for wireless networks. 6. Continuous User Authentication Using Mouse Dynamics, Machine Learning, and Minecraft

A multitude of user authentication methods have been previously proposed and are currently in use, such as fingerprint scanners, iris scanners, and voice recognition systems. Rather than utilizing physical biometrics of a user for authentication, this paper explores using behavioral biometrics, namely mouse dynamics, and machine/deep learning for user authentication. Original data was collected for this experiment and the mouse movement maps for the first three users is shown.

8. Curiosity Detection in Student Text Responses

The goal of this study is to gain insight into students’ thought process related to Computer Science course material so that faculty can develop more engaging questions and homework to improve the learning process. Students in Computer Science department courses at the University of Wisconsin – Eau Claire will be asked to take part in an approximately 30-minute session to develop question-based answers in response to a series of prompts. The process used in the session is based on the Question Formulation Technique developed by the Right Question Institute.

12. Application of Machine Learning Prediction in Psychology

A majority of the field of Psychology is focused on the explanation of human thoughts and behaviors rather than predicting future human behaviors. By applying machine learning techniques, researchers have been able to produce relatively accurate predictions of human behavior such as future drug abuse, future injurious behavior and clinical risk developments. The goal of this study is to analyze previous works as well as apply machine learning in a future model pertaining to a topic of Psychology.
